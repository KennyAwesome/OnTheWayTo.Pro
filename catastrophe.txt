One might feel resistance,
when reading the words by Fyodor Dostoyevsky that
"It is better to be unhappy and know the worst,
than to be happy in a fool's paradise."
Similar quotes has also already been stated by  John Stuart Mill:
"It is better to be a human being dissatisfied than a pig satisfied;"
Even though it has been observed that other species are able to teach
their offsprings some practical knowledge how to hunt,
so far there is no documentation of other species
capable of inherit knowledge which are not immediately practical.
One might then realize that the essence of humanity is their ability
to inherit knowledge to the next generation,
which are not immediately practical.
Such knowledge might seem abstract, boring and complicated,
but it is the only way to spot the warning signs for an catastrophe.
There are two types of catastrophes.
One is inevitable catastrophe, such as dying of the sun or black holes.
The others are avoidable catastrophe.
It is necessary to accept the possiblity of both,
but have enough optimism to prepare to avoid the avoidable one.
Here are few avoidable catastrophes, and how to prepare for it:
- Solar Flares:
	- Make electronics EMP proof
	- Have spare parts inside a EMP proof casing
	- Prediction through observation
- Black out / power outage:
	- Renewable or sustainable power sources
	- Fortification of existing power grid
	- Efforts to sustain offgrid life style
- Climate change and weather extremes:
	- Less reckless consumer behaviour
	- More investment in sustainable innovations
	- More research in safe places to live
	- Fully autarkic hydroponic agricultural systems
  - Working on an artsylum
- (world) war:
	- More application of wisdom through education
	- Better means for resource distribution
	- Unification of humanity
	- Better process or improvement of operation of governments
- Artificial Intelligence/cyber warfare:
	- Remaining a humanity worth keeping alive
  - Decentralization
  - Education about cyber security
- Declining birthrate:
	- More support for caregivers
	- More education about caregiving
	- As plan B: artificial womb
- Pandemic:
	- Mention only, because it is a complicated and sensitive Topic
- Asteroid:
  - innovation in space travel
  - longterm observation of celestial bodies
- Loneliness epidemic:
	- Practice one's own ultimate art 
	- Help others
	- Participate at the annual "The Ultimate Art"-Exhibition
	- Seek professional help
