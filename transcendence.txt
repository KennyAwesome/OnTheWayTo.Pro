"Information is just bits of data.
Knowledge is putting them together.
Wisdom is transcending them."
After that quote by Ram Dass one might
ponder about transcendence to which 
Rajneesh might have something to say:
"Transcendence means going beyond duality.
Attachment means remaining within duality."
One might see a clue for transcendence in
the realization by Rabindranath Tagore:
"Only in love are unity and duality not in conflict."
One might then find an inspiration in the
realization by Bertolt Brecht:
"Love is the desire to give, not to receive, something."
One might then appreciate the advice by Timothy Keller:
"Generosity is not only about money.
There is more than one currency.
Let your generosity be pervasive in life."
Those who wonder what currency to give
might find inspiration in the words by Ben Parr:
"Attention is the most important currency that
anybody can give you.
It’s worth more than money, possession, or things."
One might appreciate the following warning by David Allen:
"If you don’t pay appropriate attention
to what has your attention,
it will take more of your attention than it deserves."
Those who are not aware what has their attention
might appreciate the following advice by Bryant McGill:
"The love and attention you always thought
you wanted from someone else is
the love and attention you first need to give to yourself."
Those who cannot stop seeking for attention of others
might appreciate the realization by Denis Waitley:
"People who seek attention, need all the help they can get."
One might find help by saying:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
