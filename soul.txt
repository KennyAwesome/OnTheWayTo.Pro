Someone once said:
"Understand me. I'm not like an ordinary world.
I have my madness,
I live in another dimension and 
I do not have time for things that have no soul."
One thus may ponder what that entity called "soul" may be.
It is immeasurable with the state of the art scientific equipment,
but yet its existence is undeniable
given the inhumane experients in human history.
One might find peace in the teaching of gurus specialized in metaphysics.
Others might find peace in the teaching of science fiction
such as in the movies called "Ghost in the Shell".
If one is truely searching for one's own soul,
one may appriciate the advice that
one might find it during mindful activities,
such as meditation, workouts or
activities which requires balance,
such as bouldering, surfing or slacklining.
Eventhough one may not be able to define "soul",
one might be able to describe it as in:
"Your core values are the deeply held beliefs that
authentically describe your soul."
One might appreciate the following advice to find
an authentic description of one's own soul: 
"Soul is about finding the things in your life that are real and pure."
Those who has not found what they may claim as pure
might appreciate the following words:
"Beauty is the purest feeling of the soul.
Beauty arises when soul is satisfied."
Those who wonder what may satisfy one's soul
might appreciate the following realization:
"The purest form of listening is to listen without memory or desire."
One might find appreciate the clarity in these words:
"The difference between hearing and listening is paying attention."
One may then appreciate the following words:
"Paying attention is the most basic and profound expression of love."
One might appreciate the following definition:
"A soulmate is the one person whose love is powerful enough
to motivate you to meet your soul,
to do the emotional work of self-discovery,
of awakening."
One might find a soulmate by saying:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
