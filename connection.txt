One might encounter an ability or property
which could be referred as "connecting".
Those might then regard to that connection as art,
since life would not be possible without connection of atoms.
Once one has understood the concept of connection,
one may be tempted to call oneself and others an "artist".
One might then feel the desire to devote all of their
effort to improve their art as Oscar Wilde realized:
"It is through art, and through art only,
that we can realise our perfection."
Those might appreciate the potential of an event called
"The Ultimate Art" where one may find an occation to connect
with others to whom one might refer as an artist.
One may even get to a state of exploration for inspirations to speak,
knowing that someone is listening nearby,
who will be as much exited as you are,
when hearing what you have to say.
One might be inspired to call it the "ultimate connection".
But there might be those who feel the desire to hide,
especially the true emotions.
Unexpressed emotions might decrease the motivation
to keep seeking for the ultimate connection in others and by others.
Thus it is essential to accept the unexpressed parts of oneself and
the others with compassion,
which is one of the "four elements of true love" described in Zen.
One might then appreciate the encouragement by Brené Brown:
"Believing that you’re enough is
what gives you the courage to be authentic."
Those who do not know how to believe in oneself,
 might appreciate the following mantra:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
