One might encounter an essential ability for survival
which could be referred as "connecting".
Those might then regard to that connection as art.
One may then even dares to call oneself an artist.
One might feel the desire to devote all of their
effort to improve their art as Oscar Wilde realized:
"It is through art, and through art only,
that we can realise our perfection."
One might appreciate the potential of an event called
"The Ultimate Art" where one may find an occation to connect.
One may even get to a state of exploration for inspirations to speak,
knowing that someone is listening nearby,
who will be as much exited as you are,
when hearing what you have to say.
One might be inspired to call it the "ultimate connection".
Those who are trying to find the courage to
opening one's own heart  to authentically express 
all the emotions one is feeling,
in spite of the possibility of rejection,
may appreciate the words by Brené Brown:
"Courage starts with showing up and letting ourselves be seen."
It is human nature to wanting to hide,
especially the true emotions,
since it is the precondition for a civilization to exist.
But if one hides ones emotion, especially the negative ones,
others will sense that surpression.
Unexpressed emotions will decrease the motivation
to keep seeking for the ultimate connection in others and by others.
Thus it is essential to offer and accept
the expression of the unexpressed of oneself and
the others with compassion,
which is one of the four elements of true love.
One might appreciate the encouragement by Brené Brown:
"Believing that you’re enough is
what gives you the courage to be authentic."
One might appreciate the following mantra to
convince oneself to be enough by saying out loud:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
