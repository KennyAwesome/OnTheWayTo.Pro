Albert Camus realized:
"Nobody realizes that some people
expend tremendous energy merely to be normal."
One might find courage in the words by A.G. Howard:
"Normal is subjective.
Don't ever let anyone tell you you're not normal."
But one might still be left with the question,
why the modern human society oustracizes deviation of individuals,
by declaring them "insane".
This ostracization might be an unconscious safety mechanism against mass insanity,
which Dean Koontz might have wanted to warn:
"But insanity is everywhere these days, and celebrated.
Insanity is rapidly becoming the new normal."
Through progress in psychology and psychiatry it is possible to
differentiate between "insanity" and just being "unreasonable".
As pointed out by George Bernard Shaw:
"The reasonable man adapts himself to the world;
the unreasonable one persists to adapt the world to himself.
Therefore all progress depends on the unreasonable man."
The adaptation to what is regarded as "normal" might become fatal,
as warned by Grace Hopper:
"The most dangerous phrase in our language is,
'we've always done it in this way."
One may fear change
as history has taught us how quick it might wreak havoc.
But as Albert Einstein already pointed out:
"We cannot solve our problems
with the same thinking we used when we created them."
One might wonder how one might introduce change,
to which Tony Robbins realization might be helpful:
"Change happens when the pain of staying the same
is greater than the pain of change."
It could be an explanation why one might be tempted by suicide,
just to end the pain of staying the same.
So the imperative demand by society to be "normal",
might be one of the many causes of the increase in global suicide rate.
As Henri Bergson already realized:
"To exist is to change,
to change is to mature,
to mature is to go on creating oneself endlessly."
One needs to create oneself,
and once one is creating oneself to keep on living,
one can be regarded as an artist.
An ideal artist could be defined as someone who is
constantly in the state of flow during creation.
The ultimate artist can be defined as the first artist,
who defined the "The Ultimate Art".
A Pro artist could be defined as someone who is striving to be
an ideal artist and for that the following mantra might be useful:
"We are all onthewayto.pro"
