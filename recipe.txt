Nutrition is a highly controversal topic,
because the concept of "health" is based off of
the absence of diseases.
So depending on whatever diseases you may have,
what is heathy for you differs.

But to give a rough guideline,
here is a recipe for the ultimate buddha bowl.
It is designed to minimize the necessity for
diversity of plants to grow yet to be nutrionally compleate
and to prevent currently most common causes of death:
1) Cardio vascular diseases: Do not use oil.
Use foodsafe silicone container and cook rice, lentis
and optionally quinoa with carrots and shitake mushrooms with water
inside a electrically self regulated pressure cooker.
2) Diabetes: Do not use sugar. Do not overeat. Calculate your NEAT.
In that same silicone container, put tofu and marinate it
with one part soy sauce and one part vinear. Optionally add chilli.
The marinade can be used as sauce on the rice mixture.
3) Cancer: Add broccoli sprouts at the end.
4) Depression: Add kimchi before eating. Add 20g of ground walnuts for DHA.
5) Organ failure: Be aware that the plants don't contain toxins and heavy metals.
