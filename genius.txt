The word "genius" has one origin in latin meaning "creative force",
which is undoubtedly one aspect of all art.
But as every force, that force can reap havoc, if it is left unmastered.
To prevent damage caused hubris of the narcissitic interpretation of "genius",
humilty would be advisable.
To prevent damage caused by naive playfulness of individuals with talents,
humanity is adviced to have enough resources to educate them.
Humanity is advised not to be naive about the containment of those individuals,
as mentioned in the warning by Aristotle:
"There is no great genius without some touch of madness."
To prevent damage caused by neglegence of nurture the curiousity lying dormant in all of humanity,
one can find inspiration and courage to explore and master oneself by saying:
"We are all onthewayto.pro"

that the author was in fact genius and not just insane as famously quoted Salvador Dalí eloquently stated:
"There is only one difference between a madman and me. The madman thinks he is sane. I know I am mad."
