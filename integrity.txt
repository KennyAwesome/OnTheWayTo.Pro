One might appreciate the words by Margaret Thatcher:
"Choice is the essence of ethics:
If there were no choice there would be no ethics,
no good, no evil; 
Good and evil have meaning only insofar as man is free to choose."
For those what to choose,
might appreciate the words by Lajos Kossuth:
"Ethics may be defined as the obligations of morality."
One might then agree to the interpreation by Charlie Munger:
"We have a high moral responsibility to be rational."
For those who feel resistance towards the words by Charlie Munger
might appreciate the realization by Zygmunt Bauman:
"The rationality of the ruled is always the weapon of the rulers."
One might then realize the absurdity of morality through
the words by Theodore Dreiser:
"Morality and ethics are nothing but footballs,
wherewith people, strong people play to win points."
One might appreciate the wisdom by M.H. McKee:
"Wisdom is knowing the right path to take.
Integrity is taking it."
One might appreciate the understanding of
integrity by David Jeremiah:
"Integrity is keeping a commitment
even after circumstances have changed."
One might understand the reason for the importance of
keeping a commitment through the words by John C. Maxwell:
"When you make a commitment, you create hope.
When you keep a commitment you create trust!"
One might appreiate the understanding of trust by Simon Sinek:
"Trust has two dimensions: competence and integrity.
We will forgive mistakes of competence.
Mistakes of integrity are harder to overcome."
One might understand how not to make a mistake of integrity
through the words by Nicole Guillaume:
"Everyone makes mistakes,
but only a person with integrity owns up to them."
John Wooden understood that it is not enough to
own up to ones own mistakes:
"A mistake is valuable if you do four things with it:
recognize it, admit it, learn from it, forget it."
Those who struggle to forget the mistakes
might find it helpful to to repeat the following mantra:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
