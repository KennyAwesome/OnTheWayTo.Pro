One might find oneself overwhelmed
by the phenomenon called life.
Those might appreciate the following wisdom :
"Tactics is knowing what to do,
when there is something to do.
Strategy is knowing what to do
when there is nothing to do."
One might then wonder about the essence of strategy.
One might find an answer in these words:
"The essence of strategy is choosing what not to do."
To console those who did survive without a strategy,
one might find solace in the following words:
"I'm not
heartless.
I've just learned how to use my
heart less."

One might ask whether it is sufficient to have just a strategy,
for whom the following words might be the answer:
"Strategy without tactics is the slowest route to victory.
Tactics without strategy is the noise before defeat."
The only known strategy which kept humanity alive
so far might be found in the following proverb:
"If you want to go fast,
go alone.
If you want to go far,
go together."
One can only hope to be fortunate enough to find a company 
by keep reciting the following mantra:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
