Some people may try to justify war by saying:
"The only excuse for war is that we may live in peace unharmed."
But those who understand that the essence of justice is merely vengence, as in the movie called "V for Vendetta" might realize that "true" atonement to restore the original state might be impossible.
That realization might trigger a series of negative emotion such as dispair in some humans, which might end up in resentment, if untreated. One might find peace in a treatment which might result in forgiveness exemplified in this quote:
"Forgiveness is accepting the apology you will never receive."
One might then appreciate the understanding in that quote that forgiveness requires more than just acceptance:
"Forgiveness is not a feeling; it is a commitment. It is a choice to show mercy, not to hold the offense up against the offender. Forgiveness is an expression of love."
Those who seek to show such mercy might appreciate these words:
"Mercy was more frightening than murder, because it was harder."
The reason why mercy might be harder than murder might be understood in these words:
"Evil feeds on mercy."
THose who seek to understand whatever might be evil might appreciate these words:
"No man chooses evil because it is evil; he only mistakes it for happiness, the good he seeks."
Those who seek to understand whatever might be good, might appreciate the keen observation in the following statement:
"'For the greater good.' That has been the excuse of tyrants throughout all time."
One might be tempted to give up on the effort to define "good" as a consequence and resort to other vaguely defined concepts such as:
"That which is done out of love is always beyond good and evil."
One might then realize that any definition is incomplete as in:
"To define is to limit."
After that realization one might then appreciate the following realization:
"I am conscious of my own limitations. That consciousness is my only strength."   
Those who seek to become conscious of their own limitations might appreciate these words:   
"History is written by the winners."
One might then appreciate the encouraging definition of a "winner":
"You’ve got to find a way to survive because they win when your soul dies."
The definition of soul may vary due to its metaphysical nature, but there is undeniably something within oneself described as:
"Death is not the greatest loss in life.
The greatest loss is what dies inside us while we live."
For those who feel like something has died inside oneself, might appreciate the following words of encouragement:
"As long as you're still alive, you always have the chance to start again."
One may then find oneself feels stuck in a perpetual cycle waiting for the chance to start again. In case of which one might appreciate the wisdom:
"Your life does not get better by chance, it gets better by change."
One might then appreciate the advice on how to change:
"The secret of change is to focus all of your energy not on fighting the old, but on building the new."
To build whatever is "new" one might appreciate the following words:
"Everything can be taken from a man but one thing:
the last of the human freedoms— to choose one's attitude in any given set of circumstances, to choose one's own way."
When confronted with the choice of one's own way, one might feel resistence as in the following description:
"Resistance is experienced as fear; The degree of fear equates to the strength of resistance."
One might agree to the following quote on what the strongest kind of fear might be that one is resisting:
"The oldest and strongest emotion of mankind is fear, and the oldest and strongest kind of fear is fear of the unknown."
One might appreciate the following wisdom  to find a clue to overcome that fear:
"Thinking will not overcome fear, but action will."   
One might appreciate the following clue on what such an action might be:
"Only those who will risk going too far can possibly find out how far one can go."   
Those who seek to understand such risk might appreciate these words:   
"Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing."    
Those who seek to know, what they are doing, might appreciate these words:   
"Either define the moment or the moment will define you."
One might apply that knowledge and define every moment of their own life as "good".
Those who have realized the potential of that definition might appreciate the following definition:
"A true artist is not one who is inspired, but one who inspires others."
Those who seek to inspire might appreciate the following mantra:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
