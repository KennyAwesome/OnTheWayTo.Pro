One might appreciate the keen observation in the following statement:
"'For the greater good.'
That has been the excuse of tyrants throughout all time."
One might be tempted to give up on the effort to define "good"
as a consequence and resort to other
vaguely defined concepts such as:
"That which is done out of love is always beyond good and evil."
One might then realize that any definition is incomplete as in:
"To define is to limit."
After that realization one might then
appreciate the following realization:
"I am conscious of my own limitations.
That consciousness is my only strength."
One might appreciate the following realization:
"History is written by the winners."
One might then appreciate the encouraging definition of a "winner":
"You’ve got to find a way to survive
because they win when your soul dies."
The definition of soul may vary due to its metaphysical nature,
but there is undeniably something within oneself described as:
"Death is not the greatest loss in life.
The greatest loss is what dies inside us while we live."
For those who feel like something has died inside oneself,
might appreciate the following words of encouragement:
"As long as you're still alive,
you always have the chance to start again."
One may then find oneself feels stuck
in a perpetual cycle waiting for the chance to start again.
In case of which one might appreciate the wisdom:
"Your life does not get better by chance,
it gets better by change."
One might then appreciate the advice on how to change:
"The secret of change is to focus all of your energy
not on fighting the old, but on building the new."
To build whatever is "new" one might appreciate
the following words:
"Everything can be taken from a man but one thing:
the last of the human freedoms—
to choose one's attitude in any given set of circumstances,
to choose one's own way."
When confronted with the choice of one's own way,
one might feel resistence as in the following description:
"Resistance is experienced as fear;
The degree of fear equates to the strength of resistance."
One might agree to the following quote on
what the strongest kind of fear might be that one is resisting:
"The oldest and strongest emotion of mankind is fear,
and the oldest and strongest kind of fear is fear of the unknown."
One might appreciate the following wisdom 
to find a clue to overcome that fear:
"Thinking will not overcome fear,
but action will."
One might appreciate the following clue on what such an action might be:
"Either define the moment or the moment will define you."
One might apply that knowledge and define
every moment of their own life as "good".
Those who have realized the potential of that definition
might appreciate the following definition:
"A true artist is not one who is inspired,
but one who inspires others."
Those who seek to inspire might appreciate the following mantra:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
