One might appreciate the keen observation by Brandon Sanderson:
"'For the greater good.'
That has been the excuse of tyrants throughout all time."
As a concequence one might be tempted to give up
on the effort to define "good" and
resort to other vaguely defined concepts as Friedrich Nietzsche:
"That which is done out of love is always beyond good and evil."
One might then realize that any definition is incompleate,
as realized by Oscar Wilde:
"To define is to limit."
One might appreciate the realization by Mahatma Gandhi:
"I am conscious of my own limitations.
That consciousness is my only strength."
One might appreciate the following realization by Napoleon,
who also might also have had that strength:
"History is written by the winners."
One might then appreciate the encouraging definition of a "winner"
by Tupac Shakur:
"You’ve got to find a way to survive because they win when your soul dies."
