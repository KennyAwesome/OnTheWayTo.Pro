One might appreciate the keen observation by Brandon Sanderson:
"'For the greater good.'
That has been the excuse of tyrants throughout all time."
One might be tempted to give up on the effort to define "good"
as a consequence and resort to other
vaguely defined concepts as Friedrich Nietzsche tried to do:
"That which is done out of love is always beyond good and evil."
One might then realize that any definition is incomplete
as realized by Oscar Wilde: "To define is to limit."
After that realization one might then
appreciate the realization by Mahatma Gandhi:
"I am conscious of my own limitations.
That consciousness is my only strength."
One might appreciate the following realization by Napoleon,
who also might also have had that strength:
"History is written by the winners."
One might then appreciate the encouraging definition
of a "winner" by Tupac Shakur:
"You’ve got to find a way to survive
because they win when your soul dies."
The definition of soul may vary due to its metaphysical nature,
but there is undeniably something within oneself
as described by Norman Cousins:
"Death is not the greatest loss in life.
The greatest loss is what dies inside us while we live."
One way to surive is to hold on that something within oneself,
which can be simply done by
proactively keep defining ones own life as "good".
One might appreciate the following words of encouragement by Emily Acker:
"As long as you're still alive,
you always have the chance to start again."
One might then appreciate the advice
by Louise Hay to figure out how to start again:
"Resistance is the first step to change."
One might appreciate the understanding of resistance
by Preston Norton:
"Hope is an act of resistance."
Those who are in search of what to hope for
might appreciate the advice by Albert Camus:
"The only way to deal with an unfree world
is to become so absolutely free that
your very existence is an act of rebellion."
One might the appreciate the clarification of
what to hold on to by Viktor Frankl:
"Everything can be taken from a man but one thing:
the last of the human freedoms—to choose one's attitude
in any given set of circumstances,
to choose one's own way."
One might find one's own way through the study
of the following mantra:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
