There is a german proverb "Order is half of the rent."
One may wonder, what would be the other half would be, 
if it would not be money, which would be obviously implied in that sentence.
One may appreciate the guess that it might be be stability,
if stability is defined as the execution of order.
One may then be able to define order as the knowledge of the patterns resulting in stability.
Stablity is hence the absence of chaos,
which essence might be understood as the unknown and the unexpected.
So order is a state in which everything has its place and
that everything is at its place.
One thrive for the exploration of the unknown,
stability might seem boring and sterile as in the following statement:
"The Daoist answer is right on the line,
where you have one foot in order so that you’re fairly stable,
and you have another foot in chaos so that new and interesting and
compelling and transforming things are happening to you."

Thus one may appreciate the undestanding that
one may not be able to thrive only through order and that
one may be in need of chaos as a source of inspiration.
For those feeling drowning in chaos, 
the following words might be encouraging:
"Creativity is the ability to introduce order into the randomness of nature."
