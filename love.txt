The word "love" can be understood in many different ways.
It would be censorship to limit the way it could be understood.
However it does not mean that one should neglect the effort to
deepen the understanding of the word love,
given one dares to claim to be an artist, as Vincent van Gogh said:
"There is nothing more truly artistic than to love people."
Even the highly regarded psychiatrist concluded that:
"Love is the ultimate and the highest goal to which man can aspire."
To love is the essence to be onthewayto.pro
With that realization, one might still be asking for the essence of love.
The only way to deepen the understanding of love
without limiting the essence of love is the constant effort to improve 
the description of ones experience of love, as Søren Kierkegaard claimed:
"Life is not a problem to be solved, but a reality to be experienced."
Here are a collection of descriptions which might help you:

One might feel intuitive resistance, when Bertolt Brecht dared to define love,
with his following quote:
"Love is the wish to give, not to receive, something.
Love is the art of producing something with the other's talents."
The reason for this resistance might be caused by disappointment by those whom one might have loved.
Thus it is inevitable to seek for "true love".
Thich Nhat Hanh, a Zen Master, named the "four elements of true love",
and here is a short summery of his explaination:
1. Meinhu = Brotherhood love (discover the need of others = understanding)
2. Karuna = Compassionate love (try helping their suffering)
3. Mudita = Joyful love (enjoy together, effort to offer joy in times of need)
4. Upeksha = Non discriminatory love (Inclusive and calm love)
 
As an honorable mention the ancient greek philosophers
distinguished six forms of love.
The 3 well known distictions of love:
1. Eros = Romantic love
2. Philia = Friendly love
3. Agape = Unconditional love
But there are also lesser known forms of love:
4. Philautia = Self love
5. Xenia = Guest love
6. Storge = Familial love

There is a modern approach by Matthew Hussey describing four stages of a relationship:
1. Admiration (Unrequited love)
2. Mutual attraction (Not only limited to Eros)
3. Commitment (marriage, having/raising children)
4. Compatibility (The unnegotiable things are not a burden to each other.)

For those who seek clarity regarding whether someone loves oneself,
but is indecisive whether the other is ready for a confrontation,
words by Warren Wiersbe might be helpful:
"Truth without love is brutality, and love without truth is hypocrisy."
If one is hurting oneself through unrequited love through exploitation,
it would be advisable to confront for self love.
If one thinks one has recognised all the elements of true love for the other,
one need to understand, that one lacks the understanding,
whether the confrontation is helping the other.
And ultimately, helping others is the only action which is benevolent,
and remaining silent until the "right" timing to protect the peace of others,
might be the only kind way.
During that excruciating period of self restraint,
one might appreciate the words by George Savile:
"A man who is a master of patience is master of everything else."

For those suffering from a heart break,
the following two quotes might give the feeling of firstly being understood,
and secondly how to recover from it:

"The most painful state of being is remembering the future,
particularly the one you'll never have."
(Søren Kierkegaard)

"Art is to console those who are broken by life."
(Vincent Van Gogh)

As long as you keep remembering that we are all onthewayto.pro,
there will be hope to find the ultimate connection,
to make you feel less lost and lonely.
