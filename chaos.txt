There is a german proverb "Ordnung ist die halbe Miete." (= Order is half of the rent.)
I have been wondering, what would be the other half, 
if it would not be money, which would be implied in that sentence.
I realized, that it would be stability,
because stability is the execution of order.
And order is the knowledge of the patterns how to create stability.
Stablity is the absence of chaos,
which is in essence the unknown and the unexpected.
So order is a state in which everything has its place and
that everything is at its place.
For artists who thrive for the exploration of the unknown,
stability might seem boring and sterile.

"The Daoist answer is right on the line,
where you have one foot in order so that you’re fairly stable,
and you have another foot in chaos so that new and interesting and
compelling and transforming things are happening to you." (Jordan Peterson)

Thus artists cannot thrive only on order.
One needs chaos as a source of inspiration.
