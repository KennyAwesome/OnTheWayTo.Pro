Whatever prevents one's own suicide can be regarded as the "ultimate" philosophy.
But even if such philosophy existed,
it would probably not prevent the killing of another person with the intent of war,
because it would probably vary for each individual.

One solution approach might be to first redefine "art" as something
which inspires oneself to keep on living voluntarily.
Then one might redefine "ultimate" art as follows:
"The 'ultimate' art is expressed in the most simple and
genuine way after one has just helped another by saying:
We are all on the way to Pro."

Anyone can simply admire their own "ultimate" art in every day life,
which is the most effortless action done in the state called "flow",
which has required diligence and dedication to improve.

Another way to admire the "ultimate" art of others is the annual offline display event.
"The Ultimate Art" will be displaying a video collage of the "ultimate" art of artists,
who voluntarily submitted a minimal report of the progress they have made.
The video should either contain the final few moments of compleation the artwork and
the following smug in joy of archievement,
the skill one has worked on to improve
or any avaiable documentation of the artwork.
It is not a essencial requirement,
but the effort to say the following mantra at the end will be appreciated:
"We are all on the way to Pro."

The understanding that there may be many reasons,
why a submission might not be shown in the final stream will be appreciated.
