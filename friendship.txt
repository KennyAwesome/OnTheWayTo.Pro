While internalizing the three lessons of history.txt,
one may contemplate about how to maintain longterm peace.
The conscious agreement not to harm each other preceeds self control,
which is in essence impulse control and the knowledge and execution of "good manners".
Both can be summed up as politeness.
But politeness can be misused to frown upon others.
Thus it is essential to recognize and admire with humility
the effort of others, who strive to be polite,
which is the essence of honor.
There may be occations where honor is not sufficient,
because they may ask you for help or guidance.
In that case one should have the courage to offer help.
Eventhough one way to be helpful is to be honest,
to maintain good friendship one should not forget the following quote:
"Truth without love is brutality, and love without truth is hypocrisy."
So before one dares to speak the truth, 
one should ask oneself the questions formed as following:
"Before you speak ask yourself if what you are going to say is true, is kind, is necessary, is helpful.
If the answer is no, maybe what you are about to say should be left unsaid."
The quote should be considered as an encouragement to strive towards more compassion and understanding.
It is rare to find good friendship,
thus the only way left is to excercise diligently to love those,
whom one can call a friend.
As it has been already stated:
"Hope for the best, prepare for the worst."
It is inevitable to encounter someone, who acts willfully against the mentioned virtues.
One should be prepared for reclusion from them,
because one cannot help those, who do not want to be helped.
But one should not give up the hope on them that they might
desire to change themselves.
The expression of that hope, one should maintain an open channel
capable to listen to them when they are ready.
To excersise compassion until they are ready,
the following mantra might be helpful:
"We are all on the way to Pro."
