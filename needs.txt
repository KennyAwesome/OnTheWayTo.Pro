Despite of the critisim, one might find the "hierarchy of needs" by Abraham Maslow useful.
To inherit its value to the next generation, here is short explaination of the subjective understanding,
written by the author of this document.
In midst of capitalism Marslow came up with the idea of "hierarchy of needs",
to explain the needs beyond the physiological needs, 
which is food, water, rest and warmth.
He explicitly mentioned warmth, because shelter would be a need one level higher in the hierarchy,
which is security.
To security one may count job with prospect until artsylum is fully operational, 
long term rent, emergency savings, insurance, etc.
After those two materialistic need are satisfied,
one might have enough capacity to explore the need for "friendship and belonging".
There is no general order in which those needs should be adressed,
but one can decrease the amount of unnessecary suffering, by taking one step at a time.
Once one has found a place and people one can call home,
one may furhter solidify and protect that home by asking help by those who are not immediate aquaintances.
To prepare oneself for that occasion of emergency, one should not neglect building up "esteem".
In the understanding of the author of this document, "esteem" is, how one is perceived by others,
because of established standards.
At that stage, one might be pondering how to be regarded in high "esteem".
One of the most simple ways to do is to practive a martial arts with a belt system.
But one is advices not to remain in convenient complaisance only because one has reached the maximal possible capability in one domain of life.
One is rather encouraged to max out all the other capablities as well, which in it's ultimate state is regarded as "self actualization".
After one humbly realizes that one has self actualized enought, so that their daily life is not dictated by survival,
one might feel "Weltschmerz". With that realization, further explained in "effort.txt", one might feel the volition to contribute to humanity,
which is the lesser known last layer of the hierarchy of needs called "self transcendance".
But be well adviced: Avoid sacrificing yourself devoting for others, when that sacrifice does not come from a place of abundance.
This document should be regarded as a warning as well as encouragement to contribute to humanity by expressing your "ultimate" art.
