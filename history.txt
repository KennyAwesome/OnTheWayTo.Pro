Of all subjects which can be taught,
history might be the most difficult but also
the most important to teach.
For one, what is considered as "truth" is controversial,
because winners write history, hiding their atrocities.
For one, the content is highly traumatizing.
For one, those who need it's lessons most,
will perceive it as boring, if they haven't experienced war.

To save time and convey the message of history as a subject,
one can use the following three lessons from history as a guiding virtue:
First lesson of history will be gratitude.
With the awareness of what one had to go through to survive,
one will feel gratitude towards your own ancestors.
Second lesson will be respect.
With the awareness of what others had to go through to survive and
what atorcities they are capable of,
one will feel respect towards others and their the ancestors.
The third lesson will be peace.
With the awareness of what horrible atrocities one and
the others are capable of, it will be inevitably neccesary,
to agree to not to do those atrocities to each other.

At last but not least, the following quote will make clear of
one's obligation to active effort for deepening the
understanding for history:
"Human nature is evil, and goodness is caused by intential activity." (Xun Kuang)

But don't be discouraged by our capabality of evil,
as long as we are united as artists,
we will be capable of good as well.
